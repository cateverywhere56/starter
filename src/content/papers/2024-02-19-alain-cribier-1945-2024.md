## L’essentiel

Alain Cribier, the pioneering cardiologist who performed the world’s first transcatheter aortic valve implantation (TAVI) in 2002, has died at the age of 79. Cribier, who had been the director of cardiology at Charles Nicolle Hospital, University of Rouen (Rouen, France), was recognised globally for his role in advancing the treatment of aortic disease.
