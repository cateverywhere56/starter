## L’essentiel

A recent study published in Circulation concerning patients with chronic limb-threatening ischaemia (CLTI) in the USA, has found that patients of low-income status and those who attended “safety-net” hospitals—facilities that treat patients regardless of insurance status or ability to pay—were more likely to receive low-intensity vascular care, which was associated with decreased long-term event-free survival.
